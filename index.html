Roots

My roots in Northern California run six generations deep—before California became a state.

I come from a long line of makers and community builders, people whose hands shaped the places they called home. That tie to the land gives you something: a strong sense of place. It also asks something: an ecological ethos of land stewardship. I believe this is where my systems thinking began, at the DNA level.

"Making" in our family has always been a form of memory, a form of care.

Kincraft grows from this same soil. Every pattern carries the colors of a specific place on a specific day. Every hidden message carries the story of that place—its climate, its ecology, the people who love it. Crafting the future, one maker at a time.

As a social scientist by training, I am also infinitely curious in the ways humans come together to organize for a common cause. From interdisciplinary collaborations and the overnight creation of infrastructure following a crisis, to the formation of networks that drive social change. My research has taken me from futures thinking to ethical AI to transnational networks—and now home, to Kincraft and the maker community.
